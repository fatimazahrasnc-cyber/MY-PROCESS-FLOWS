01 — Overview

What is this project?

This is an ongoing project where you describe any business or operational process in plain language, and I generate a fully designed, color-coded HTML flowchart — exactly like the Purchase Department flowchart — that you can open in any browser, print, or share.

No design tools needed. No formatting effort on your part. Just describe the process clearly and I handle the rest.

🏢

Any Department

Works for any business process — finance, HR, operations, sales, IT, compliance, or custom workflows.

👥

Multi-Role Flows

Each person or team gets their own color-coded swim lane so responsibilities are immediately clear.

🔀

Decisions & Loops

Approval gates, yes/no branches, revision loops, and conditional paths are all handled automatically.

📄

Editable HTML File

You receive a single .html file — open in browser, print to PDF, or edit the source code as needed.

02 — How It Works

Simple three-step cycle

The workflow is straightforward. You describe, I build, you receive a file.

1

You describe the process

Tell me the process in plain language — who does what, in what order, what decisions are made, and any special rules or notes. No special format required; conversational descriptions work perfectly.

2

I design the flowchart

I assign each role a swim lane with a unique color, map out every step and decision, add cross-lane connectors, highlight important rules, and build a clean HTML file with professional typography and hover animations.

3

You download the file

You receive a ready-to-use .html file. Open it in any browser. Print it. Share it. Edit the text directly in the file if needed. You can also ask me to make corrections or additions at any time.

03 — Your Input

What to tell me

For the best result, your description should cover the following information. The more clearly you describe it, the more accurate the flowchart will be.

Information What to Include Type
People / Roles Names or titles of every person or team involved. E.g. "Raza (Purchaser)", "Mr. Shahid (Business Partner)", "Store Manager". Required
Steps in order What happens first, second, third — and who does each step. Even a rough narrative order is fine. Required
Decision points Any yes/no gates or approval steps. E.g. "If approved, proceed. If not, return to Raza for revision." Required
Special rules or notes Important constraints, warnings, or exceptions. E.g. "Rate on invoice may differ from PO rate." Optional
Reports or outputs Any reports, documents, or data that the process produces. E.g. daily report, discrepancy log, filed invoice. Optional
Process name What to call the process — used as the flowchart title. E.g. "Sales Order Process", "HR Onboarding". Recommended
Department name Which department or area this belongs to. Used in the header. E.g. "Accounts Department". Recommended
💡
You do not need to use any special format. Just write the process as you would explain it to a colleague. I will extract the structure, identify the roles, and map the flow automatically.

05 — Example

Example of a good prompt

Here is the style of description that produces the best results, based on the Purchase Department example you already used.

✅   Good example — Purchase Department prompt
Purchase Department: Purchase Order will be created by Purchaser (Raza) and for that if new inventory item is to be added, that would also be added by Raza. The PO would then be approved by Mr. Shahid (Business Partner) in software and then the purchasing can be done by Raza. Once stock will be received by Waqas (Store Manager) he will copy the approved purchase order to a new purchase invoice against which stock is received. After that, the purchase invoice entered in the system should be printed and attached with bill provided by supplier in physical file by Waqas. The rate should be same as on purchase invoice/bill received from supplier, not necessarily same as it was initially estimated on purchase order. The accounts department (Numan) will check the entire work done by Raza and Waqas closely — whether the inventory item is created correctly, quantity and rate is matched with Purchase Order or not, whether the PO was approved against which purchase made — and will share daily Purchase order reports. The purchase orders report will include: Pending Purchase Orders, Completed Purchase Orders, and a Discrepancy Report for completed orders where invoice price was higher than approved PO price.
✅
Notice: this prompt names each person with their role in brackets, describes the flow in sequence, mentions the decision point (approval), states the special rule (rate may differ), and describes the final report. That's all I need.
06 — Output

What you will receive

Every flowchart I create for you includes the following components as standard.

🏊
Color-coded swim lanes — one per role Each person or team gets their own vertical lane with a unique color. Responsibilities never overlap visually.
📦
Numbered step cards Every action in the process is a clearly labeled card with a step number, title, and description inside the correct person's lane.
◆
Decision diamonds Approval gates and yes/no branches are shown as decision nodes with YES/NO path indicators clearly labeled.
↔️
Cross-lane connectors When a step passes from one person to another, a labeled connector arrow shows the handoff clearly.
⚠️
Warning / note banners Any special rules, exceptions, or important constraints are highlighted in an amber warning banner.
📊
Reports & outputs section If the process produces any reports or documents, they are shown in a dedicated section at the bottom.
💾
Downloadable .html file A single self-contained file you can open in any browser, print, share, or open in a text editor to edit.
07 — Design

Color assignment system

Colors are assigned automatically to roles in the order they appear in your process description. The first role gets purple, the second blue, the third teal, and so on. The accounts/audit role always gets red when present.

🟣
Role 1
Purple — primary actor
🔵
Role 2
Blue — approver / reviewer
🟢
Role 3
Teal — receiver / executor
🔴
Audit / Accounts
Red — oversight role
🎨
If you have more than 3 roles (excluding audit), I will assign additional colors automatically. You can also request a specific color for a specific role in your prompt.
08 — Standards

Rules & standards I follow

Every flowchart I create follows these standards consistently to ensure quality and usability.

Standard How it's applied
One lane per role Every named person or team in the process gets their own vertical swim lane. No role shares a lane.
Steps numbered sequentially Steps are numbered 01, 02, 03… in the order they occur in the process, regardless of which lane they appear in.
Decisions shown clearly Every yes/no gate is shown as a dashed-border decision card with explicit YES and NO badges and path descriptions.
Rejection loops shown If a step can be rejected and returned to a previous actor, this loop is shown with a distinct red dashed visual indicator.
Cross-role handoffs labeled When a task passes from one person to another, the connecting element includes a short label describing what is being handed over.
Special rules highlighted Any important constraint, exception, or rule is shown in an amber warning banner, never buried in step text.
Audit/oversight at the bottom If an accounts or oversight role reviews the entire process, their section spans the full width at the bottom of the chart.
Print-ready output The HTML file includes print styles so it renders cleanly when printed from the browser or saved as PDF.
09 — Quick Reference

Quick reference summary

Everything you need to remember

  • Describe any process in plain language — no special format needed
  • Name each person with their role in brackets, e.g. "Raza (Purchaser)"
  • Mention decision points explicitly — "If approved… if not…"
  • Include any important rules or exceptions as special notes
  • Mention any reports or documents the process produces
  • End your message with: "Please create an HTML flowchart for this process."
  • You will receive a downloadable .html file — open in any browser
  • Ask for corrections anytime — I will update and regenerate the file
  • You can also ask for a PDF, Word doc, or inline visual preview
  • For complex processes, describe one sub-process at a time for clarity
